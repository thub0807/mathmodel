# Legacy Reference: Writing and Assembly

This file now supports `paper_generation_protocol.md`.

## Knowledge To Keep

- Write from audited and locked evidence, not from memory.
- Draft the abstract late, after the paper body is stable.
- Reuse per-question summaries rather than re-deriving the story from raw code outputs.
- Keep figures, tables, notation, and claims aligned.
- Do not introduce new quantitative results during writing.

## Helpful Writing Sequence

1. problem restatement
2. analysis and decomposition
3. assumptions and notation
4. per-question methods and results
5. verification and sensitivity
6. evaluation and extension
7. references and appendices
8. abstract and final pass

## Writing Reminders

- Pull competition-specific phrase banks, abstract templates, and paper skeletons only when they help the current section.
- Treat winning patterns as inspiration, not as a replacement for evidence.
