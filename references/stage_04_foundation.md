# Legacy Reference: Assumptions, Notation, and Terminology

This file now supports `per_question_plan.md`, `verification_protocol.md`, and later paper drafting.

## Knowledge To Keep

- Keep assumptions explicit, limited, and supported.
- Number assumptions clearly, such as `A1`, `A2`, `A3`.
- Prefer three to seven material assumptions rather than none or an unreviewable long list.
- Give every symbol a meaning and unit, including unitless quantities.
- Keep terminology consistent across planning, code, validation, and writing.

## Reuse in v1.2

Use this material to strengthen:
- `assumptions.md`
- `notation.md`
- `validation.md`
- `paper.md`

## Good Practice

- Mark whether an assumption is model-driven, data-driven, or environment-driven.
- Add one support line for each important assumption.
- Keep notation synchronized with both formulas and data-field names.
- Avoid symbol reuse for different meanings.
